1970s - Herbie Hancock

Our musician for the 1970s is Herbie Hancock!

Herbie Hancock was a pianist and innovator of the jazz fusion style of jazz.

Practitioners of the jazz fusion style sought to fuse jazz with funk, R&B and rock music. Jazz fusion arose in the 1970s, when new technologies like the synthesizer and electric guitar opened up a new realm of possible sounds that jazz musicians could incorporate into their music.

Herbie Hancock, who held a degree in electrical engineering, was fascinated with the new technology and was one of the first jazz musicians to incorporate it into his music. He formed a band called The Headhunters in 1973 to explore these new sounds. That band produced many important jazz fusion records throughout the 1970s, many of which are now considered must-haves by jazz aficionados today.
